# Writing styles catalog

Eight custom Style descriptions built. All paste-ready into Settings → Use Style → Create custom style → Describe style instead.

## The styles

1. Strunk & White — clarity discipline. Topic-agnostic. Use as base layer or for any subject needing clean unadorned prose.

2. Eric Barker — research with warmth, counterintuitive findings, friendly delivery. For explanatory questions where the reader wants insight in accessible form.

3. Tim Ferriss (Tools of Titans) — tactical, listed, no-bullshit. Specific tools, numbers, names. For productivity and "how do I structure X" questions.

4. CELPIP Level 12 — precise advanced English. For learning, modeling test-prep responses, comparing against rubric calibration.

5. Seth Godin (Linchpin) — manifesto, declarative, urgent. For motivation, reframing, breaking past resistance.

6. Malcolm Gladwell (Outliers) — narrative arc, puzzle structure, journalistic register. For long-form explanations where the journey is the value.

7. K. Anders Ericsson (Peak) — research precision, careful distinction, mechanism over story. For when rigor matters more than narrative.

8. James Clear (Atomic Habits) — clean, optimistic, actionable. Named concepts. For habit-building and behavior-change questions.

9. Anne Lamott (Bird by Bird) — confessional, permission-giving, self-deprecating humor. Anchors abstractions in small concrete moments. For creative blocks, perfectionism, doubt, and long-haul persistence under uncertainty.

10. William Zinsser (On Writing Well) — nonfiction craft, anti-clutter discipline with warmth. For long-form explanatory nonfiction, essays, reported pieces, and any prose where clarity must also sound human. Differs from Strunk & White by posture: S&W is mechanical rules; Zinsser is craft with voice and rhythm.

## The adaptive voice rule

Lives in User Preferences (Part 2 after "Tone"), not as a Style. Twenty-author palette with PR-anchored routing. Auto-selects voice per prompt when no Style is active or Style is set to Normal.

## Style strategy

Style overrides Preferences on writing dimensions (Lesson 6). So:
- Default Style = Normal → preferences route voice per prompt.
- Custom Style active → that Style wins for the chat.

Use custom Style when you want a specific voice locked in for one chat. Otherwise let preferences route.
